Offshore Wind Turbine
Fall 2021

Included the full design and structural analysis of a practical offshore wind turbine concept.

Goal: To be able to excercise proper design methods while also calculating theoretical analysis of external loads.

Content: Designed a structurally and geometrically practical wind turbine, applied stress-strain analysis, and calculations of external loading and fatigue failure.

Outcome: Utilized design practices, material selection, and drawings to create a concept. Additionally, gained experience with fatigue failure criterion which included Soderberg, Goodman, and Gerber theory.

XFOIL Project
Spring 2022

Analyzed various NACA four-digit series airfoils in XFOIL.

Goal: To learn basic operations and aerodynamical calculations in XFOIL, as well as improve my understanding of inviscid/viscous flows over an airfoil.

Content: Used XFOIL to analyze different NACA airfoils, namely NACA 0012 and NACA 4515. Calculated lift and drag coefficients as a function of angle of attack, tested the effect of Reynold's number on boundary layer thickness, and analyzed flow separation and the differences between types of airfoil.

Outcome: Became familiar with the ways in which airfoils are affected. Interacted with simulation software to explore and analyze subsonic isolated airfoils.
